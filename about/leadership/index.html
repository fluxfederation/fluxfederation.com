Flux Board

Nic Kennedy
Chair

Nic's been the Chair of Flux Federation from May 2018, and was previously an Independent Director at both Powershop and Flux. She likes to dream big and empower others to do the same. She's passionate about supporting women in business and technology, and helping Kiwi companies to punch well above their weight internationally.

Nic has significant technology, business transformation and strategic planning experience, coming from executive and leadership roles in both startup and corporate environments. She specialises in scaling technology startups globally and is currently the Chief Innovation & Ventures Officer for PwC. Previously, as Vend's Chief Delivery Officer, she lead and scaled the product and engineering teams, overseeing a strong growth period while building a top-notch team and an internationally recognised product.

Before Vend, Nic scaled teams at Orion Health and worked in startups and multi-national corporates in Holland and the UK. Nic is also an Independent Director for The Mind Lab. She is a Chartered Member of the NZ Institute of Directors.

Jason Stein
Director

Jason joined the Flux Board in 2018 and brings with him governance, legal and people skills, with a strong interest in the people aspect of business.

Jason is Meridian's General Manager of the Office of the Chief Executive. Catherine Reynolds
Director

Catherine is an experienced Board Director and Advisor for high growth, entrepreneurial companies in the tech and professional services sectors, and is excited to join Flux on its growth journey. She has a passion for helping executive teams to deliver on long-term vision by balancing today's priorities with the need to plan using hard metrics, executive accountability, empathy, humility and intuition.

She has experience in building governance and strategic growth frameworks around disruptive, technology centric ventures, as well as strategic "market making" expertise around the customer in start-ups, high growth SMEs, blue chip and multinational organisations.

Catherine's strengths lie in the technology industry specifically where bleeding edge innovation requires the scale of enterprise partners and smart capital to grow and thrive in highly regulated environments. She is attracted to industries that are "in flux" and has been a pioneer throughout her career. She was one of the early pioneers of Business Process Engineering (before it existed) in the 1980s, convergent media (mobile telco and media) in the late 1990s, federated identity and web services in the early 2000s, customer centric data analytics and transactional services in the late 2000s and most recently AI for the professional services sector.
